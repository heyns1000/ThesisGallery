Background on the Patentability of Business Methods

 

The patentability of computer software- and business method-related inventions is a grey area and differs from country to country.

 

Section 25(2) of our Patents Act excludes certain subject matter from patentability including methods of doing business, programs for computers and the presentation of information. However, this exclusion is qualified by Section 25(3) which effectively states that the exclusion applies only to the extent to which the invention relates to that subject matter as such. These sections parallel Article 52(2) and Article 52(3) of the European Patent Convention.

 

At present, there is no case law in South Africa interpreting the meaning of these sections. Our courts may follow a European-type approach requiring a "technical effect" as laid down by the Boards of Appeal of the European Patent Office or our courts may adopt a different, less formal approach as has been done in other countries. In Europe, the invention should in essence provide a technical solution to a technical problem (i.e. not a business problem).
